Q7
Netflix – Analytics Use Cases
Descriptive Analytics:
Business problem: Netflix wants to understand how the platform performed in the previous month.
Data used: Viewing history, watch time, number of active users, genre preferences, device usage.
Expected output: Reports showing total hours watched, most popular shows, top genres, and peak usage time.
Business value: Helps Netflix understand customer behavior, measure performance, and identify trending content for future planning.

Diagnostic Analytics:
Business problem: Subscription cancellations increased in some regions and Netflix wants to know the reason.
Data used: Cancellation records, user complaints, buffering logs, content availability, customer feedback.
Expected output: Identification of root causes such as poor streaming quality or lack of new content.
Business value: Enables Netflix to fix issues quickly, improve service quality, and reduce customer churn.

Predictive Analytics:
Business problem: Netflix wants to predict which users are likely to cancel their subscription soon.
Data used: Login frequency, watch history, past churn data, subscription activity, complaints.
Expected output: Churn probability score or list of high-risk users.
Business value: Helps in taking early retention actions like offers or personalized recommendations to prevent revenue loss.

Prescriptive Analytics:
Business problem: Netflix wants to decide the best actions to increase engagement and retain customers.
Data used: Predictive results, user preferences, recommendation data, pricing information, A/B testing results.
Expected output: Suggested actions such as recommending specific shows, sending discounts, or personalized notifications.
Business value: Improves user satisfaction, increases retention, and maximizes revenue.
